## Setting Up Your First Instagram Ad Campaign

### Prerequisites

Before running ads, you need:
1. **Instagram Business Account** (not personal account)
2. **Facebook Page** (required to run Instagram ads)
3. **Meta Ads Manager Account** (freeâ€”sign up at business.facebook.com)
4. **Payment Method** (credit/debit card linked to Ads Manager)

### Step-by-Step Campaign Setup

#### Step 1: Choose Your Objective

1. Go to Meta Ads Manager
2. Click "Create" â†’ "Ad"
3. Select objective (start with "Leads" for your first campaign)
4. Name your campaign (e.g., "Buyer Leads - Austin - May 2025")

#### Step 2: Define Your Audience

**Location:**

- Radius: 25 miles
- Or specific zip codes/neighborhoods

**Demographics:**
- Age: 25-45 (adjust based on your niche)
- Gender: All (unless targeting specific demographic)
- Language: English (or add Spanish if applicable)

**Detailed Targeting:**
Click "Edit" under Detailed Targeting and add:
- **Interests:**

  - Home buying

  - New job

- **Income Level:**
  - Top 10-25% of zip code (for luxury)

**Audience Size:**
Aim for 50,000-500,000 people. Too narrow (<10K) = high costs. Too broad (>1M) = wasted spend.

**Pro Tip:** Create separate audiences for buyers vs. sellers.

#### Step 3: Choose Ad Placements

**Recommended for Beginners:**
- Instagram Feed
- Instagram Stories
- Instagram Reels

**Uncheck:**
- Facebook Feed (unless you want to run there too)
- Audience Network
- Messenger
- Facebook Marketplace

**Why?** Focus on Instagram only to start. You can expand later.

#### Step 4: Set Your Budget & Schedule

**Budget Options:**
1. **Daily Budget:** Instagram spends up to this amount per day
   - Start with $10-20/day
2. **Lifetime Budget:** Total amount for entire campaign
   - Example: $300 for 30 days

**Schedule:**
- **Start:** Today or future date
- **End:** Set end date or run continuously

**Pro Tip:** Start with a 7-day test at $15/day ($105 total) to gather data before scaling.

#### Step 5: Create Your Ad Creative

**Ad Format Options:**

**1. Single Image Ad**
- One photo with caption
- Best for: Listing ads, testimonial screenshots

**2. Carousel Ad**
- 2-10 images users swipe through
- Best for: Property tours (one room per slide)

**3. Video Ad**
- 15-60 second video
- Best for: Virtual tours, agent introductions, testimonials

**4. Stories Ad**
- Full-screen vertical ad (9:16 ratio)
- Best for: Quick listing highlights, announcements

**Image/Video Requirements:**
- **Resolution:** Minimum 1080x1080 (square) or 1080x1920 (vertical)
- **File Size:** Max 30MB for images, 4GB for videos
- **Text Overlay:** Keep minimal (Instagram penalizes image ads with >20% text)

**Pro Tip:** Use high-quality listing photos or professional video. Blurry phone photos kill performance.

## Creative Best Practices for Real Estate Ads

### Photo Ads

**What Works:**
- Exterior shot with blue sky
- Bright, well-lit interiors
- Luxury features (pools, kitchens, master suites)
- Before & after renovations
- Happy clients with "SOLD" signs

**What Doesn't Work:**
- Dark, poorly lit photos
- Cluttered rooms
- Photos with heavy text overlay (Instagram penalizes this)
- Stock photos (use real listings)

**Design Tips:**
- Use Canva to add subtle branding (logo in corner)
- Add property price in text overlay (small, bottom corner)
- Ensure photo is high resolution (1080x1080 minimum)

### Video Ads

**What Works:**
- 15-30 second property tours
- Agent introduction videos
- Client testimonials
- Neighborhood walkthroughs
- Educational tips (e.g., "3 mistakes first-time buyers make")

**What Doesn't Work:**
- Static slideshows (use carousel instead)
- Shaky phone footage
- Poor audio quality
- Videos longer than 60 seconds (attention drops)

**Editing Tips:**
- Add captions (85% watch without sound)
- Use trending music (from Instagram's library)
- Include CTA text in final 3 seconds
- Keep first 3 seconds attention-grabbing

### Carousel Ads

**What Works:**
- Property tour (one room per slide)
- Neighborhood guide (each slide = different amenity)
- Before & after transformations
- Listicles (e.g., "5 Tips for First-Time Buyers")

**Structure:**
- Slide 1: Exterior + headline
- Slide 2-5: Best interior features
- Slide 6: Backyard/outdoor space
- Slide 7: CTA (contact info or link)

**Pro Tip:** First image matters most. Use your best photo to hook scrollers.

## Advanced Instagram Ads Strategies

### 1. **Retargeting Warm Audiences**

Most people don't convert on first exposure. Retarget people who:
- Visited your Instagram profile
- Engaged with your posts
- Clicked a previous ad but didn't convert
- Visited your website

**How:**
1. Create a Custom Audience in Ads Manager
2. Choose "Instagram Profile" or "Website Visitors"
3. Set timeframe (e.g., last 30 days)
4. Run ads specifically to this warm audience

**Ad Creative:** Offer a different incentive or address objections. Example: "Still thinking about selling? Here are 3 reasons now is the perfect time."

**Expected Results:** 2-3x higher conversion rate than cold audiences.

### 2. **Lookalike Audiences**

Instagram finds new people similar to your best clients.

**How:**
1. Upload a list of past clients (email addresses)
2. Create a "Lookalike Audience" in Ads Manager
3. Instagram finds people with similar demographics, interests, and behaviors
4. Run ads to this audience

**Expected Results:** Higher quality leads at lower cost.

### 3. **A/B Testing (Split Testing)**

Test different variables to optimize performance:
- **Creative:** Image A vs. Image B
- **Copy:** Headline A vs. Headline B
- **Audience:** Age 25-35 vs. Age 35-45
- **Placement:** Feed vs. Stories

**How:**
When creating an ad, toggle "A/B Test" and select what to test.

**Run For:** 7-14 days with at least $10/day per variant

**What to Do:** Keep the winner, turn off the loser, test something new.

### 4. **Video Views Campaigns**

Run video ads to build awareness, then retarget video viewers with lead ads.

**Strategy:**
1. Run "Video Views" campaign at $10/day for 7 days
2. Create Custom Audience of people who watched 50%+ of video
3. Run "Leads" campaign to that warm audience

**Why It Works:** People who watched your video are warmer leads.

### 5. **Dynamic Ads (Advanced)**

Automatically show personalized ads based on user behavior.

**Example:** Someone views a $500K listing on your website. Instagram automatically shows them an ad for that exact listing.

**Requires:** Meta Pixel + product catalog setup (complexâ€”for advanced users)

## Common Instagram Ad Mistakes (And How to Fix Them)

### 1. **Targeting Too Broad**

**Mistake:** Targeting "everyone in the US interested in real estate"

**Fix:** Narrow to your local market with income and life-event targeting.

### 2. **Low-Quality Creative**

**Mistake:** Using blurry photos or amateur videos

**Fix:** Invest in professional photography or use high-quality stock photos.

### 3. **No Clear CTA**

**Mistake:** Ad says "Check out this listing!" but doesn't tell people what to do

**Fix:** Add explicit CTA: "Click to schedule showing," "Download free guide," "DM me for details."

### 4. **Not Testing**

**Mistake:** Running one ad indefinitely without testing variations

**Fix:** Always run A/B tests. Test creative, copy, audiences, and placements.

### 5. **Ignoring Analytics**

**Mistake:** Letting ads run without checking performance

**Fix:** Review Ads Manager at least 3x/week. Pause underperforming ads, scale winners.

### 6. **Slow Follow-Up**

**Mistake:** Leads come in but you respond 24-48 hours later

**Fix:** Respond within 1 hour (ideally 15 minutes). Use CRM automation or hire a VA to handle initial responses.

### 7. **Asking for Too Much Info**

**Mistake:** Lead form with 10 fields (full address, employment history, etc.)

**Fix:** Ask only for name, email, phone, and 1-2 qualifying questions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much should I spend on Instagram ads as a real estate agent?**
Start with $10-15/day ($300-450/month) to test. Scale to $20-50/day once you're generating qualified leads consistently. Expect to spend $500-2,000 in ads to close one deal.

**What's a good cost per lead for real estate Instagram ads?**
$5-25 per lead is typical. Niche audiences (e.g., luxury buyers) may be $10-15/lead. Broad audiences in competitive markets may be $15-30/lead.

**Should I run ads to Instagram, Facebook, or both?**
Start with Instagram only to simplify. Once you optimize, expand to Facebook. The same ad campaign can run on both platforms simultaneously.

**How long should I run an ad before deciding if it works?**
Run for at least 7 days and 50-100 clicks or 20-30 leads before making a decision. Stopping too early prevents the algorithm from optimizing.

**Can I run Instagram ads without a website?**
Yes! Use Lead Ads (people submit info directly on Instagram) or drive traffic to your link in bio page. No website required.

**What's the best ad objective for real estate agents?**
**Leads** is the most effective for real estate. It captures contact info directly on Instagram without requiring a landing page.

**Do I need to hire someone to run my Instagram ads?**
No. Instagram ads are beginner-friendly. Follow this guide and you can set up your first campaign in 30 minutes. Hire an expert once you're spending $50+/day and want advanced optimization.

**How do I respond to leads from Instagram ads?**
Respond within 1 hour (ideally 15 minutes). Call, text, or email leads with a personalized message. Use a CRM to track and automate follow-up.

**Can I retarget people who visited my Instagram profile?**
Yes! Create a Custom Audience of "People who visited your Instagram profile" and run ads specifically to them.

**How do I know if my Instagram ads are working?**
Track cost per lead, consultation booking rate, and deals closed from ads. If you're spending $500 in ads and closing a $10,000 deal, your ads are working.
